# Portal

Portal is a dedicated space within Nicholas Tritsaris' retro web collection, serving as a gateway to experiments, side projects, and creative explorations. Designed with a nostalgic aesthetic, Portal acts as a launchpad for new ideas and interactive content, connecting visitors to different projects, archives, and experimental pages in one place. This project is part of a broader retro web ecosystem, featuring a unique blend of _retro-themed_ design elements and interactive content.

## Overview

Portal is designed to match the nostalgic vibe of the main site, with a _retro theme_ that incorporates early-2000s web design elements. The project is intended to be a **gateway** to various experiments, side projects, and creative explorations, providing a single point of access to a wide range of content. By navigating through sub-pages, visitors can discover unique content and explore the different projects and archives that are part of the Portal.

## Features

The key aspects of Portal include:

* **Gateway Function**: Connecting visitors to different projects, archives, and experimental pages in one place.
* **Retro Theme**: Styled with early-2000s web design elements to match the nostalgic vibe of the main site.
* **Exploration**: Visitors can navigate through sub-pages to discover unique content.
* **Part of the Collection**: Portal is integrated into the broader retro web ecosystem, alongside projects like Net Atlas Ultimate, Net Observation Project, and MC Property Encyclopedia.
