EchoZone is a *location-based*, **anonymous** social media platform that enables users to engage in *real-time* discussions with their local community. Inspired by [Yik Yak](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yik_Yak), EchoZone allows users to post short messages that are visible only within a defined *geographic radius*, fostering a sense of community and anonymity. This platform is currently in the *early development* stage, with a planned tech stack that includes **React**, **Node.js**, and **Firebase**.

## Overview
EchoZone is designed to facilitate *local discussions* and community interaction, providing a platform for users to share their thoughts and ideas without revealing their identities. The platform's *location-based* feed ensures that users can engage with content that is relevant to their surroundings, making it an ideal tool for *community engagement* and *social interaction*.

## Features
The core features of EchoZone include:
- **Anonymous posting**, allowing users to share their thoughts without fear of retribution
- *Location-based feed*, which displays posts from users within a defined geographic radius
- **Upvotes and downvotes**, enabling users to interact with and moderate content
- *Real-time updates*, ensuring that users can engage with the latest posts and discussions
- **Content moderation tools**, which help to maintain a safe and respectful community

## Technical Details
The planned tech stack for EchoZone includes:
- **Frontend**: *React* and *React Native* for building the user interface
- **Backend**: *Node.js* and *Express* for handling server-side logic
- **Realtime Data**: *Firebase* and *WebSockets* for enabling real-time updates
- **Maps & Location**: *Mapbox* for providing location-based services
- **Authentication**: *Anonymous* and *device-based* authentication methods

## Status
EchoZone is currently in the *early development* stage, with a focus on building the core features and tech stack. As the platform continues to evolve, it is expected to provide a unique and engaging experience for users, facilitating *local discussions* and *community interaction* in a safe and respectful environment.
