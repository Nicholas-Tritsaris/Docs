# nk missile tests

### Summary

The `nk-missile-tests` repository provides an _interactive visualization_ of flight tests of all missiles launched by **North Korea** from 1984 to 2026. The visualization also includes the real-time positions of North Korea's satellites on a globe. The data for this visualization is sourced from [the CNS North Korea Missile Test Database](http://www.nti.org/analysis/articles/cns-north-korea-missile-test-database/), which documents all missile tests capable of delivering a payload of at least 500 kilograms a distance of at least 300 kilometers.

### Overview

The `nk-missile-tests` repository offers a unique perspective on **North Korea's missile program** by visualizing the flight tests of all missiles launched since 1984. The visualization allows users to explore the data in an interactive and intuitive way, with features such as rotation, zooming, and filtering.

<a href="https://nagix.github.io/nk-missile-tests" class="button primary">Live Demo</a>

### Features

The `nk-missile-tests` repository includes the following key features:

* Interactive globe visualization of missile flight tests
* Real-time positions of North Korea's satellites
* Rotation, zooming, and tilting of the globe
* Filtering by test outcome (success, failure, unknown)
* Filtering by missile name
* Search functionality by date or test name
* Histogram view of test outcomes
* Information on the database and references available through the ABOUT button

### Usage

The visualization can be interacted with in the following ways:

* Mouse drag or swipe: Rotate the globe
* Mouse wheel rotation or pinch in/out: Zoom in/out
* Click or tap +/- buttons: Zoom in/out
* Shift key + Mouse drag or three-finger swipe: Tilt up/down
* Click or tap the tilt buttons: Tilt up/down
* Click or tap a test label on the map: Select a test
* Enter a date or test name in the text field: Search for a test
* Click or tap ABOUT button: See the information on the database and references
* Click, tap or drag the timeline bar: Select a year
* Click or tap the graph icon: Show or hide the histogram
* Click or tap SUCCESS, FAILURE and UNKNOWN buttons: Filter tests out by outcome
* Click or tap missile check marks: Filter tests out by missile name

### About Data

The data for this visualization is sourced from [the CNS North Korea Missile Test Database](http://www.nti.org/analysis/articles/cns-north-korea-missile-test-database/), which is the first database to record flight tests of all missiles launched by **North Korea** capable of delivering a payload of at least 500 kilograms a distance of at least 300 kilometers. The database captures advancements in **North Korea's missile program** by documenting all such tests since the first one occurred in April 1984, and will be routinely updated as events warrant. Additionally, bearing information from other sources such as the **Japan Ministry of Defense** is used to calculate landing locations.
